# Daily PM Execution & Rituals Guide

## Overview

This guide provides step-by-step instructions for day-to-day PM execution in large organizations. These are the "Tuesday morning" guides‚Äîpractical, actionable steps for what to do right now.

## Step 0: Braindump & Assess (Critical!)

**Before setting up your routines, braindump:**
- What does your typical day look like? Dump everything - don't judge yet.
- What does your product sense tell you? What's working? What's not?
- What assumptions are you making about how you should work?
- What biases might affect your routines? (Firefighting? Meeting overload?)

**Product sense exercise:**
- If you had to be 2x more effective, what would you change?
- What would make you say "this routine is obviously wrong"?
- What would make you say "this routine is obviously right"?

### STEP 2: Time Block by Context

**Don‚Äôt mix contexts in same time block**

## üéØ Common Daily Scenarios

### Scenario: Everything Feels Urgent

**When your day is all firefighting:**

**STEP 1: Triage (10 minutes)**

```

1. [Item]
2. [Item]
3. [Item]

- Urgent AND Important? ‚Üí Do now
- Urgent, NOT Important? ‚Üí Delegate or defer
- Important, NOT Urgent? ‚Üí Schedule properly
- Neither? ‚Üí Delete/ignore

- Could someone else handle it?
- What happens if I don't do this today?
```

**STEP 2: Create forcing function**

```

[THE ONE THING]

Everything else is secondary.

Why this one thing?
- [Impact if done]
- [Cost if not done]

```

### Scenario: Stuck and Don‚Äôt Know What to Do Next

### Scenario: Meeting-Filled Day, No Focus Time

**When calendar is 100% booked:**

